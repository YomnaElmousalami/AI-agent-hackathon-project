[03:00] Worked example:
(Scenario walkthrough)
Q: Scenario: You're driving your own car to school, then later you start using the same car to deliver food on weekends. What's the key insurance difference?
A: Personal auto usually covers normal personal driving (school, errands). If you use the car to make money (deliveries/rideshare), you may need commercial coverage or a specific endorsement—otherwise a claim could be denied.

[05:30] Recap: Recap: personal = everyday driving. commercial = business driving. The 'why' is risk: business driving changes how much and why you drive.
